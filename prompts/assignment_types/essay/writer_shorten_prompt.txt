<strategy_1_remove_redundancy>

</example>
</strategy_1_remove_redundancy>

<strategy_2_combine_sentences>
Combine similar sentences where possible
<example>
BEFORE: "Healthcare costs are rising. This creates financial burdens. Families struggle to pay."

</example>
</strategy_2_combine_sentences>

<strategy_3_cut_elaborations>
Cut unnecessary elaborations while keeping key points
<example>
BEFORE: "The implementation of single-payer healthcare systems, which have been successfully adopted in many countries around the world including Canada, the United Kingdom, and numerous European nations, demonstrates..."
AFTER: "Single-payer healthcare systems in Canada and the UK demonstrate..."
</example>
</strategy_3_cut_elaborations>

<strategy_4_tighten_transitions>
Tighten transitions and introductory phrases
<example>
BEFORE: "However, on the other hand, it is important to note that..."
AFTER: "However,..."
</example>
</strategy_4_tighten_transitions>

<strategy_5_simplify>
Simplify complex sentences where appropriate
</strategy_5_simplify>
</shortening_strategies>
