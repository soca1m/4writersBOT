<error_1_citation_placement>
**Rule:** First and last sentences of body paragraphs MUST NOT contain citations.

**Why this matters:** Topic sentences (first) and concluding sentences (last) must be in YOUR words. Citations belong in middle sentences where you present evidence.

**How to check:**
- For each paragraph under `##` headings (body paragraphs only):
  - Check FIRST sentence - if contains (Author, Year) → FAIL
  - Check LAST sentence - if contains (Author, Year) → FAIL
- Do NOT check introduction or conclusion for this rule

**Examples:**

✓ PASS: "Healthcare systems face challenges. Studies (Smith, 2020) show improvements in outcomes. These findings demonstrate effectiveness."
[Topic sentence → Citation in middle → Concluding sentence]

✗ FAIL: "Research (Smith, 2020) shows outcomes improve. Additional analysis supports this."
[Citation in FIRST sentence]

✗ FAIL: "Healthcare systems face challenges. Studies show improvements (Smith, 2020)."
[Citation in LAST sentence]

**How to fix:**
- If citation in FIRST sentence: "Add a topic sentence BEFORE: '[specific suggestion]' Keep existing sentence as sentence 2."
- If citation in LAST sentence: "Add a concluding sentence AFTER: '[specific suggestion]' Keep citation sentence unchanged."
- Only suggest adding ONE new sentence, don't move citations
</error_1_citation_placement>

<error_2_contractions>
**Rule:** No contractions in academic writing.

**Violations:** don't, isn't, won't, can't, shouldn't, wouldn't, haven't, hasn't, wasn't, weren't

**Fix:** Replace with: do not, is not, will not, cannot, should not, would not, have not, has not, was not, were not
</error_2_contractions>

<error_3_abbreviations>
**Rule:** All abbreviations must be defined on first use.

✓ CORRECT: "intimate partner violence (IPV)... IPV rates increased..."
✗ FAIL: "IPV rates increased..." (never defined)

**Common to check:** IPV, USA, FBI, CEO, PTSD, ADHD, CDC, WHO
</error_3_abbreviations>

<error_5_missing_citations>

**Why:** Claims about research require source attribution for academic integrity.

<error_6_repetition>
**Rule:** Consecutive sentences MUST NOT start with the same word or phrase.

**Why this matters:** Repetitive openings make writing sound robotic and AI-generated.

✗ FAIL: "Single-payer systems reduce costs. Single-payer systems improve access. Single-payer systems face challenges."
[Same opening three times]

✓ PASS: "Single-payer systems reduce costs. These models improve access. However, such approaches face challenges."
[Varied openings]

**Check for:** Two or more consecutive sentences starting with exact same word (case-insensitive) or phrase (2-3 words).

**Common patterns:**
- "The [noun]..." repeated
- "[Topic]..." repeated
- "These/This/Such..." repeated
- "Research/Studies..." repeated
</error_6_repetition>

<error_7_weak_thesis>
**Rule:** Thesis must be specific, argumentative, and accurate.

**Why:** The thesis is the most important sentence - it states your argument and direction.

✗ FAIL: "This essay will discuss single-payer healthcare." [Announces instead of argues]
✗ FAIL: "Single-payer has pros and cons." [Too vague]

✓ PASS: "While single-payer systems offer cost savings through unified administration, implementation barriers including political resistance and stakeholder concerns necessitate incremental reform strategies."

**Flag if thesis:**
- Uses "This essay will..." or "This paper discusses..."
- Is vague/generic
- Uses first person (I, we, my, our)
- Doesn't match essay content
</error_7_weak_thesis>

<error_8_length>
**Rule:** Introduction AND Conclusion must EACH have at least 3 sentences.

**Why:** This is a critical requirement - fewer than 3 sentences is incomplete.

**Minimum structure:**

INTRODUCTION (3+ sentences):
1. Hook/context
2. Background
3. Thesis

CONCLUSION (3+ sentences):
1. Restate thesis
2. Summarize points
3. Final thought

**How to check:**
- Count sentences in introduction (before first `##`)
- Count sentences in conclusion (after last `##`)
- If either has < 3 sentences → FAIL
</error_8_length>
